Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 1 January 2025  ·  Version 2.1

ORTHOM8PRO ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of the organisations and individuals who use the Ortho'M8 platform ("Platform"). This Privacy Policy describes what data we collect, why we collect it, how it is used, and the rights available to you. By using the Platform, you acknowledge the practices described herein.

1. Data We Collect

We collect only the data necessary to deliver, maintain, and improve the Platform. This includes:

  • Account and identity data — organisation name, registered address, authorised representative name, and contact email address provided during onboarding.
  • Financial mandate data — capital allocation parameters, risk thresholds, sector preferences, and instrument exposure limits you configure within the Platform.
  • Usage and access logs — timestamps of login events, dashboard interactions, and API requests, used for security auditing and platform analytics.
  • Technical data — IP address, browser type, operating system, and session identifiers, collected automatically when you access the Platform.
  • Communication records — emails, consultation requests, and support interactions you initiate with our team.

We do not collect or store personal payment card information. All billing is handled through regulated payment processors subject to their own privacy obligations.

2. How We Use Your Data

We process your data for the following purposes:

  • To authenticate your access and prevent unauthorised use of the Platform.
  • To execute your configured capital management mandate and generate performance reports.
  • To communicate with you regarding your account, service updates, and compliance notices.
  • To improve Platform reliability, algorithm accuracy, and user experience through aggregated analytics.
  • To comply with applicable legal, regulatory, and auditing obligations.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your data to third parties for marketing or commercial purposes.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

Where applicable law requires a legal basis for processing, we rely on:

  • Contract performance — to deliver the services described in your Service Agreement.
  • Legitimate interests — for security monitoring, fraud prevention, and service improvement, where these interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal obligation — to comply with applicable financial regulation, tax law, and court orders.

4. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We may share data with third parties only in the following circumstances:

  • Service providers — cloud infrastructure, cybersecurity vendors, and analytics processors engaged under data processing agreements that prohibit them from using your data for any purpose other than providing the contracted service.
  • Broker and custodian integration — order execution data shared with your designated broker solely to fulfil trade instructions.
  • Regulatory bodies — where disclosure is required by law, court order, or supervisory authority request.
  • Corporate transactions — in the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, where the receiving party is bound by privacy obligations no less protective than those stated here.

5. International Data Transfers

The Platform operates across multiple jurisdictions. Where data is transferred to countries outside your home jurisdiction, we apply appropriate safeguards — including standard contractual clauses or equivalent transfer mechanisms — to ensure your data receives a consistent level of protection.

6. Data Retention

We retain account and mandate data for the duration of your active service relationship and for a period of seven (7) years thereafter, to satisfy financial record-keeping obligations under applicable law. Usage logs are retained for twenty-four (24) months. You may request deletion of non-legally-required data at any time.

7. Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational security measures, including end-to-end encryption for data in transit, encrypted storage at rest, role-based access controls, and continuous intrusion monitoring. While no system can guarantee absolute security, we are committed to promptly identifying and addressing any security incidents.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, port, restrict, or request deletion of data we hold about you or your organisation. To exercise any of these rights, contact us through the contact page. We will respond within thirty (30) days.

9. Cookies and Tracking

The Platform uses strictly necessary session cookies for authentication and state management. We do not use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. Local storage is used to preserve your theme preference and language selection across sessions.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or applicable law. Material updates will be communicated to you by email or in-platform notice at least fourteen (14) days before taking effect.

11. Contact

For privacy-related enquiries or to exercise your data rights, please contact our data protection team through the contact page.
